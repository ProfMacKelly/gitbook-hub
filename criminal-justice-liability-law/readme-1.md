# README (1)

## What is this Sourcebook?

This interactive sourcebook is an [open educational resource](#user-content-fn-1)[^1]. Although it is openly licensed free to be reused and redistributed, readers should note that the contents are tailored to serve undergraduate students enrolled in Criminal Justice Liability Law at Concordia University [Wisconsin ](https://www.cuw.edu/)and [Ann Arbor](https://www.cuaa.edu/). This sourcebook includes content that (a) I authored, (b) I [adapted or remixed](https://help.oercommons.org/support/solutions/articles/42000046860-remix-a-resource), and/or (c) incorporated from other creators in compliance with copyright and licensing regulations and practices.

## How to Use This Sourcebook

### Table of Contents

Use the Table of Contents (typically available in the reader UI) to browse through chapters and sections.

### Reading Content

Click on any chapter or section to view its content. Scroll down to read the entire page.

Pages with multiple headings/subsections have their own Table of Contents on the right side of the page.

### Interactive Elements

Engage with interactive elements such as links, [annotations](/broken/pages/f621df450224269ca3eeac4ce5d8fe2f54577a5e#how-annotations-work-in-this-sourcebook), expandable widgets, videos, and other embedded content included in the sourcebook. These features provide additional context and insights — spend adequate time with them to fully benefit from the material.

<details>

<summary>How Annotations Work in This Sourcebook</summary>

This sourcebook's text includes many annotations, which are indicated when text has a dotted underline like this: [This text is annotated.](#user-content-fn-2)[^2] To read the annotation, mouse over the underlined text and click/tap. A bubble will appear and display the annotation. These annotations provide additional context and information while also decluttering the space.

Instead of traditional footnotes or endnotes, many references and citations to source content are located in these annotations.

</details>
